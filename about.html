About Me

I'm Amir Khan — a business analyst in transition from pharmacy operations to AI-driven business analytics and strategy. My 10+ years of cross-domain experience across pharmaceutical sales, purchasing, and inventory management in the UAE and India have shaped my belief that operational challenges are ultimately data problems waiting for intelligent solutions.

My professional path began as a Medical Representative in India, where I learned how markets, people, and business performance interconnect through data and human behavior. Later, in the UAE, I worked over five years in pharmacy purchasing and inventory management, managing vendor relations, procurement strategies, and supply chain operations. These experiences helped me understand how data, systems, and decision-making come together to drive business performance.

I’m currently pursuing a Bachelor of Business Administration at Manipal University Jaipur and plan to continue with an MBA in AI-Driven Business Strategy. Alongside my academic path, I’ve completed certifications in Google Advanced Data Analytics, IBM AI Engineering, and IBM Generative AI Engineering, enabling me to combine business logic with analytical intelligence to design smarter workflows and decision systems.

Current Focus

I’m currently developing a self-initiated analytics project titled BlueMart Retail Analytics — a simulated omnichannel retail business case built on a synthetic dataset I created. The project focuses on performing exploratory data analysis, generating actionable business insights, and providing strategic recommendations for improving sales forecasting, inventory optimization, and channel efficiency. This initiative is my step toward applying AI and analytics in solving real-world business problems through structured, insight-driven storytelling.

My Philosophy

I believe that analytics is not just about building models — it’s about translating business realities into data narratives that drive smarter, faster decisions. My approach is guided by three principles:

Clarity

Insights must be presented clearly — so decision-makers can act confidently, not cautiously.

Impact

Every analytical effort should lead to measurable business improvement or operational intelligence.

Scalability

Intelligent systems must be adaptable — capable of learning and evolving with business needs.
