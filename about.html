About Me

I'm Amir Khan — a Data analyst in transition from operational management to AI-driven business analytics and strategy. With over a decade of experience across pharma sales, purchasing, and inventory management in the UAE and India, I’ve seen firsthand how everyday operational inefficiencies can be transformed into data problems — and how intelligence can make business scalable.

My journey began as a Medical Representative in India, where I learned how market dynamics, psychology, and data intersect in real-world sales decisions. After moving to the UAE, I spent over five years in pharmacy purchasing and inventory operations, managing vendor relations, demand forecasting, and supply optimization. These roles gave me a practical understanding of how business systems work — and how data analytics can elevate decision-making.

I’m currently pursuing my Bachelor of Business Administration at Manipal University Jaipur and plan to continue with an MBA in AI-Driven Business Strategy. To complement this academic path, I’ve completed professional certifications in Google Advanced Data Analytics, IBM AI Engineering, and IBM Generative AI Engineering, equipping me to bridge business operations with intelligent automation and analytical insight.

Current Focus

I’m currently working on a self-initiated applied analytics project titled BlueMart Retail Analytics — a simulated omnichannel retail business where I’ve generated a synthetic dataset representing store, channel, and product-level operations. The goal is to perform exploratory data analysis, extract actionable business insights, and provide recommendations on improving sales forecasting, inventory balance, and multi-channel efficiency. This project reflects my focus on real-world analytics application rather than theoretical exercises.

Vision

My long-term goal is to become an AI-driven business strategist — someone who can combine data intelligence, business acumen, and behavioral understanding to design adaptive, automated decision systems. I aim to help organizations transition from manual reporting to self-learning business models that think, predict, and improve continuously.
